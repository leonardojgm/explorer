Poppy loves reading books, and one day, she found a story about Captain Fluff, an astronaut marmot.

“I love Captain Fluff! I want to meet her!” Poppy exclaimed.

She imagined exploring space with Fluff.

“What if we studied exoplanets together?”

“Did you know there are over 5,000 exoplanets out there? It’s like a cosmic treasure hunt happening right now!”

“Exoplanets can be super wacky! Some are giant gas balls, while others are rocky like Earth. It’s like a whole zoo of planets!”

“Some have wild weather—think acid storms and scorching heat! The diversity in the universe is full of surprises!”

“How do we find these mysterious planets? We use a cool trick called 'transit.' When a planet sneaks in front of a star, it makes the star look dimmer—like flipping the switch on a cosmic lamp!”

“About 20% of exoplanets hang out in the 'habitable zone.' This is the sweet spot for liquid water, which means… maybe life is throwing a party there!”

“On some of these planets, a day can last for weeks! Imagine long, lazy days and super short nights—perfect for stargazing!”

“Some exoplanets orbit not one but TWO stars! These 'circumbinary planets' might have the most epic sunsets ever—talk about a celestial show!”

"I feel like a space detective!"

“We can uncover secrets that help protect Earth and understand how these planets influence our climate too!”

“Take TRAPPIST-1e, for example! It’s like Earth with water! Studying it helps us understand how water behaves in different places and affects our climate.”

“Then there’s Kepler-186f, our 'Earth’s sibling!' By learning about its atmosphere, we discover how water can change over time here on our planet.”

“Let’s not forget HD 209458 b, also known as Osiris! It’s a super-hot planet with huge storms. Analyzing it gives us clues about storms on Earth!”

“And what about WASP-121b? Imagine a planet so hot that its atmosphere shines! Studying it helps us understand extreme climate phenomena right here!”

“Last but not least, there’s LHS 1140 b, which might have lots of water vapor! Analyzing its atmosphere teaches us about global warming and its effects on Earth's water.”

“With amazing telescopes like the James Webb, we’re ready to uncover the universe’s secrets. It’s like having a backstage pass to the cosmos!”

“Maybe I’ll become an astronaut and explore these worlds!”
